Aggressive Weight Cuts in Main Events.

Main events can last for five long rounds for some fighters. Athletes who cut too much weight often find themselves in tough grappling or wrestling contests and quickly become fatigued.

This could be the game plan of their opponent, who knows that if they drag their opponent into rounds four and five, they can easily take the fight away from them by tiring their bigger opponent out.

Motherhood and Fighting.

Female fighters with children might find it very difficult to balance their personal and professional lives. While their bodies may recover easily, the mental strain can be challenging

Knockout Losses and Weak Chins

Suffering several KO losses can lead to a weak chin. Many fighters develop what is known as a "glass chin" and can only take one or two good shots before crumbling to the canvas. All the damage from their previous bouts adds up over time, and these injuries can affect a fighter's performance. Usually, fighters who have been in some wars (wild fights or fighting recklessly) tend to fall into this category.

Biased Judges

Judges in certain cities, such as Las Vegas, may have biases compared to judges from other countries or commissions. Whenever a fight goes to decision in Vegas, be prepared for a potentially controversial outcome. Two different judges may interpret the same fight in different ways, leading to accusations of robbery. Keep an eye on judges with a history of scoring matchups incorrectly under the current point system, as their names often come up in news articles.

Bad Referees

Organizations typically have their own staple referees, but sometimes a new and inexperienced referee is assigned to a match. Unfortunately, as a punter, you may not know who the referee will be for a specific match. Some referees may make mistakes, such as stopping a fight too early, which is out of your control. Keep an eye out for referees with a history of bad decisions and tread lightly if you know they will be the referee for the match you bet on. Additionally, different commissions have their own sets of referees, so you may see an unfamiliar referee in a different state or country.

Weight Cuts

Bad weight cuts often lead to poor performances on fight night. They can result from illness or bad dieting, and if a fighter misses weight or struggles to make weight in the weeks leading up to a bout, it could potentially put the bout in danger of being called off. Look out for fighters with a history of bad weight cuts, as they tend not to perform as well in their bouts.

Overweight

Some fighters receive hefty penalties for being overweight, which usually results in them losing a portion of their purse. This fact can put extra mental pressure on a fighter and give extra motivation to their opponent going into a fight. Being significantly overweight is seen as unprofessional, and fights may be made into catchweight bouts or canceled altogether.
